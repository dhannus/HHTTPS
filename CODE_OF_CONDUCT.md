# Code of Conduct

## TL;DR

**Be kind. Be specific. Be open to being wrong.**

## Our pledge

We — contributors and maintainers — pledge to make participation in HumanProof / HHTTPS a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected behavior

- Use welcoming and inclusive language.
- Respect differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Accept constructive criticism gracefully.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the project.
- Show empathy toward other community members.
- Acknowledge that everyone, including yourself, is learning.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Personal attacks, derogatory comments, or insulting/derogatory comments.
- Trolling, deliberately disruptive behavior, or harassment.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information (doxing) without permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome sexual attention.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces:
- GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions
- Email communication related to the project
- Public events where someone represents the project
- Social media posts about or directed at the project

## Enforcement

Violations may be reported to:

**daniel.hannuschka@tweakz.de**

All reports are kept confidential. The maintainer will review and respond within 7 days. Consequences may include warning, temporary ban, or permanent ban from project spaces.

For maintainer misconduct, contact a trusted third party (institutional partner such as TUM, CISPA, or similar — list to follow as partnerships solidify).
